1. IntroductionThe selected stakeholder for this elicitation interview is an Ethics Committee Member. This stakeholder plays a central role in the review and decision-making process for research ethics applications. Their responsibilities include evaluating research proposals, assessing risks to participants, collaborating with other committee members, and ensuring compliance with ethical standards.In addition, Ethics Committee Members interact with multiple stakeholders throughout the process, including researchers, administrative staff, and other reviewers. This places them in a unique position to provide a comprehensive perspective on how the system should support different user needs and interactions.The insights gained from this interview will help refine system requirements, particularly those related to workflow, collaboration, and application evaluation.2. Interview Questions and Justifications1. Application Assignment Process? Question: How do you currently assign applications within your team? Is it based on bandwidth, or does a head committee member oversee this process?? Justification: Understanding the assignment process will clarify how resources are allocated and whether the system needs to support dynamic assignment based on workload.? Follow-up: When you receive applications, do you prefer to get them directly from the admin staff, or is there an interim step?? Justification: This will reveal potential bottlenecks in the current workflow and help design smoother handoff processes between administrative and committee members.2. Initial Review Criteria? Question: What specific factors do you consider when reviewing an application for the first time?? Justification: Identifying key review factors will guide the design of the application submission interface to ensure researchers provide all necessary information.? Follow-up: In what scenarios do you decide to assign one or more committee members for the initial review, and who makes that assignment?? Justification: Clarifying this will help define the system's routing logic for applications based on complexity and committee member availability.3. Clarification Requests and Approval Process? Question: Under what circumstances would you pass an application for further clarification?? Justification: Understanding the criteria for clarification will inform how the system handles communication and tracking of requests for additional information.? Follow-up: If everything is in order, what is the process for sending it to the Ethics Committee for approval, rejection, or conditional approval?? Justification: This will help clarify the workflow for moving applications to the committee, ensuring that all necessary steps are incorporated into the system design.4. Conditional Approval Communication? Question: In cases of conditional approval, what steps are taken to communicate this decision?? Justification: Clarity on communication processes will inform the design of notification features within the system, ensuring timely updates for stakeholders.? Follow-up: Do you communicate these conditions directly to the admin staff, the researchers, or both? How is this doneŃvia email, a communication hub, or face-to-face meetings?? Justification: This will help determine the systemŐs requirements for communication channels and protocols, enhancing transparency and efficiency.5. Application Status Updates? Question: During the review process, how do you keep researchers informed about the status of their applications?? Justification: Understanding current status update practices will guide the development of a status tracking feature that meets researchers' expectations.? Follow-up: What kind of updates do you think are essential for researchers to receive?? Justification: This will help identify key information that needs to be communicated, ensuring the system provides relevant updates.6. Discussion Preferences? Question: Are discussions among committee members typically held in face-to-face meetings, or do you prefer using internal channels for these discussions?? Justification: This will inform the design of collaborative features within the system, helping to ensure that communication methods align with committee preferences.? Follow-up: Which method do you find most effective for decision-making, and why?? Justification: Insights into preferred discussion methods will help prioritize features that enhance collaboration and streamline decision-making.7. Risk and Complexity Workflows? Question: Can you provide examples of how the workflow differs for low-risk, medium-risk, and high-risk applications?? Justification: Understanding these workflows will help establish the routing logic and approval processes necessary for different risk levels.? Question: If we were to implement RBAC, who should have access to the data related to each research activity? Justification: This will clarify the requirements for auditing and monitoring actions taken within the system.Group 39
