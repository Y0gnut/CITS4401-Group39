### a. Stakeholder Descriptions

1.  **Researchers (Honours, Masters, and PhD students, as well as Academic Staff)**
    - **Description:** They are the primary initiators of research activities, responsible for submitting applications, methodologies, and supporting documents for ethics review.
    - **Value:** The system provides them with a centralized portal to manage document versions and offers transparency through real-time tracking of their application’s progress[cite: 68, 71, 94].

2.  **Administrative Staff**
    - **Description:** They act as coordinators who perform initial completeness checks, track application statuses, and assign reviewers to manage the overall workflow.
    - **Value:** By replacing fragmented spreadsheets with a unified system, they can efficiently monitor all active applications and maintain a clear audit trail for university compliance.

---

### b. User Stories

**Stakeholder: Researchers**
- **user story 1:** As a researcher, I want to upload and version-control my supporting documents (e.g., consent forms) within the system, so that I can ensure the committee is always reviewing the most authoritative version.
- **user story 2:** As a researcher, I want to see the real-time status of my ethics application, so that I have transparency and can plan my research project timelines accordingly.
- **user story 3:** As a researcher, I want to define the roles and responsibilities of my team members within an application, so that their involvement is officially recorded for compliance.

**Stakeholder: Administrative Staff**
- **user story 4:** As an administrator, I want to use a digital checklist to perform completeness checks, so that I can consistently decide whether to progress an application or request missing documents.
- **user story 5:** As an administrator, I want a centralized dashboard to track the granular status of all applications, so that I can identify bottlenecks and reduce delays in the approval process.
- **user story 6:** As an administrator, I want to assign reviewers to applications based on their expertise and current workload, so that the review process is balanced and effective.
- **user story 7:** As an administrator, I want the system to restrict access to sensitive information (such as conflict of interest declarations) based on user roles, so that we maintain strict confidentiality and integrity.
